## Education

### PhD in Computational Neuroscience — Cum Laude
**Universidad Autónoma de Madrid / Instituto Cajal, CSIC**
*November 2021 – September 2025*

- Supervisors: Dr. Liset Menéndez de la Prida (Hippo-Circuit Lab)
- Funding: FPU Fellowship (Ministry of Science, Nov 2021–Nov 2022) + INPhINIT Retaining Fellowship, La Caixa Foundation (Nov 2022–Nov 2025, 4% acceptance)
- International stay: Columbia University, New York — Losonczy Lab (4 months, 2023). Designed and implemented multimodal ETL pipelines integrating calcium imaging and electrophysiology data.
- Teaching: Teaching Assistant, "Biomedical Data Management" (6 ECTS), Biomedical Engineering degree, UAM — two academic years (Jan–May 2022, Jan–May 2023). Supervised MSc and BSc final projects.

**Thesis summary:**
Investigated how hippocampal circuits organize spatial representations, focusing on cellular heterogeneity between deep and superficial sublayers of CA1.

- *Block I — Structure Index (SI):* Graph-based computational tool (k-NN) to quantify the organisation of any variable over data in high-dimensional spaces. Produces a value between 0 and 1, applicable to categorical, scalar, or vector variables without dimensionality reduction. Validated on neural data, audio classification, and computer vision. → *PLOS Computational Biology* (2024), co-first-author.
- *Block II — Parallel spatial representations in CA1:* Using calcium miniscopes in colour-coded mice, demonstrated that deep and superficial sublayers form geometrically distinct manifolds (superficial: more elliptical; deep: more circular), related to the proportion of direction-sensitive neurons.
- *Block III — Two independent maps of the same space:* By rotating the maze 180°, induced a conflict between local and global cues. The superficial layer remained anchored to the global reference (the room); the deep layer rotated with the maze. Dual-color imaging and selective chemogenetic silencing confirmed both representations coexist and operate completely independently. When CA1 is analysed without distinguishing sublayers, the information is hidden in the average. → *Neuron* (2025), first-author.

**Imperial College London — Department of Bioengineering**
*2020 – 2021*

- GPA: 80.50 / 100
- Coursed entirely online (COVID-19), concurrent with work at Instituto Cajal
- Key courses: Brain-Machine Interfaces, Computational Neuroscience, Mathematical Methods for Bioengineers, Statistics & Data Analysis, Neuroscience, Reinforcement Learning, Biomedical Imaging
- **MSc Thesis (86.34, 40/55 credits):** *"Neural manifold approach to hippocampal spatial navigation"* — First application of the neural manifolds framework to hippocampal spatial navigation. Demonstrated that CA1 population activity organises into a low-dimensional manifold encoding position (continuous) and direction (discrete) simultaneously. Identified a generalizable position gradient across environments. Direct precursor to the doctoral thesis.
- **Award:** Stella Bagrit Centenary Memorial Prize — best MSc project cohort-wide, Imperial College London (November 2021)
